What the heck is currency arbitrage?

Webster's defines "arbitrage" as "the nearly simultaneous purchase and sale of securities or foreign exchange in different markets in order to profit from price discrepancies." Team Bling, however, defines a Webster's definition introduction as "trite and stale, at best; hack and disgusting, at worst."

So what, REALLY, is currency arbitrage?

Let's tell a tale of 4 brothers: Sean, Steven, Sam and Sue. Sean, the oldest, has been hoarding GI Joes since he was young. Steven, the 2nd oldest, has been doing the same with Barbies. Sam, one step above the baby, collects Transformers. The baby, Sue, is you!

Now let's say Sue (furthermore referred to as "You") has gotten his hands on a stash of Transformers. However, you want more (of course). Sean has been eyeing your Megatron; you, however, have been trying to get more. He has 2 less-desired Joes that you've got your eye on, and, joy of joys, he's willing to trade! You make the trade, 1 Transformer for 2 GI Joes. Then, Steven catching wind, offers you 3 of his Barbies for your dope Joes. You agree. Great! Finally, Sam, not wanting to be left out, gets in on the action and wants to trade you your Barbs for 2 of his Transformers. Do you agree? OF COURSE, you started the day trading 1 Transformer, only to end it with 2! This is how currency arbitrage works (in a hyper-simplified way).
